Megan is a skilled producer, shooter, editor and writer with experience reporting across the U.S. and from 16 countries around the world. Megan is currently a reporter, producer and shooter for PBS NewsHour Weekend. Her recent report on retail generic drug prices won a Gracie Award and National Headliner Award, and her report on the debate over GMO seed farming in Hawaii won a first-place National Headliner Award for environmental reporting. Previously Megan produced for the PBS newsmagazine Need to Know. Her report on health reform in Massachusetts won a first-place National Headliner Award and was nominated for an Emmy. Her story on disparities in the tax code also won a first-place National Headliner Award. From 2008 to 2010, Megan produced, filmed and edited for the daily PBS international news show Worldfocus. She received a Gracie Award for Liberia's Long Road Back, a series she produced with correspondent Lynn Sherr. Megan has also shot and produced for the weekly news series NOW on PBS, Teaching Channel and the WNET documentary series Treasures of New York. Megan received a Student Academy Award, Gracie Award and grant from the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences for Ladies of the Land, her documentary film about women farmers. Prior to her career as a journalist, Megan was a communications director, legislative aide and researcher on Capitol Hill. She holds a BA cum laude from Wellesley College in International Relations and French, and an MA in Broadcast Journalism from New York University. She lives in New York City.
