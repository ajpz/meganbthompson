As a correspondent for PBS NewsHour Weekend, Megan reports on everything from criminal justice reform to climate change. She also shoots, edits and serves as a substitute anchor.

Previously, Megan worked for the PBS series Need to Know, Worldfocus, NOW on PBS, and Treasures of New York. She has also produced for Bloomberg TV and Teaching Channel.

She's been nominated for three Emmys, and won four Headliner Awards and three Gracies for reporting on a range of issues from the price of generic drugs to women in Liberia to disparities in the U.S. tax code. She won a Student Academy Award for her documentary film about women farmers. In 2015, she was awarded the Roslyn Carter Fellowship for Mental Health Journalism, for which she reported on toxic stress and girls in the criminal justice system.

Prior to her career in journalism, Megan was a communications director, legislative aide and researcher in Washington, DC. She holds a BA cum laude from Wellesley College in International Relations and French, and an MA in Journalism from New York University. She lives in Manhattan with her family.
